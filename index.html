FIT3179 Data Visualisation 2

This visualization demonstrates information about primary and secondary education in Australia.

Schools and Staff Allocation

The symbol map gives an illustration of the number of schools and their staff in all states over Australia.

The brightness of red determines the number of school staff. And the size of red determines the number of schools.

The annotation of the states name is presented next to the dot.

The information about specific dot including the state, the number of schools and the number of schools staff can be examined by generating a tooltip when hovering over a red dot.

The graph shows that education resources are mainly focus on the three states, which are New South Wales, Victoria and Queensland. New South Wales has the most resourceful teaching supplies that the schools and the staff are concentrated around this state.



Number of Enrolled Students

The stacked bar chart shows that the number of enrolled student in all primary and secondary schools by states in Australia from 2009 to 2018.

The filter right below the graph can be used to filter out enrolled students by states to remove some of the states in the data and only show the specific one to visualize.

When hovering over a bar, a tooltip will be generated to show the relevant information of that particular bar.

Fluctuations are minimal in each state, with enrollment students numbers varying by a small amount from year to year. Overall, there is a slight upward trend for the enrolled students in all primary and secondary schools from 2009 to 2018. Parents and children have valued primary and secondary education at about the same level within the last decade.



Number of Enrolled Students in Government Schools

The line chart demonstrates the trend of the number of enrolled students in government schools by each state in Australia over years.

When hovering over a bar, a tooltip will be generated to show the relevant information of that particular bar.

After about a decade, the number of students has remained almost unchanged.



Number of Enrolled Students in Different Types of Schools

The grouped bar chart illustrates the number of enrolled students in both government and non-government schools by each state in Australia in 2018.

When hovering over a bar, a tooltip will be generated to show the relevant information of that particular bar.

By clicking on the name of the types of schools in the legend, the graph will highlight the selected bars and inofrmation of the specific type of school in the visualisation.

Non-government schools are more popular than government schools in all of the states within Australia, especially in states with high population density.
